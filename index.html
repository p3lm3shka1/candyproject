PROJECT CANDY

Candies have a long and fascinating history that dates back thousands of years. The earliest forms of candy were not like the colorful sweets we know today. Ancient civilizations such as the Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans used natural sweeteners like honey to coat fruits and nuts. Honey was considered a luxury, and these primitive candies were often reserved for the wealthy or for religious offerings.

The idea of candy began to evolve with the discovery and spread of sugar. Around the 6th and 7th centuries, sugarcane was cultivated in India and later spread to the Middle East and Europe through trade. By the Middle Ages, sugar was extremely expensive and rare, so it was used mostly for medicinal purposes. Apothecaries created sugar-coated herbs and spices, believing that sugar helped preserve health while making bitter medicines easier to swallow.

During the 17th and 18th centuries, as sugar production became more common and less costly, candies became available to a broader population. Europeans began experimenting with boiled sugar to create hard candies, lozenges, and even early forms of caramel. By this time, candy had shifted from being purely medicinal to being enjoyed as a treat. It became especially popular at fairs, markets, and festivals, where sellers displayed colorful sweets to attract children and adults alike.
